Infinite Banking:
Be Your Own Banker

R. Nelson Nash's landmark book Becoming Your Own Banker introduced a concept that changes everything: using dividend-paying whole life insurance from mutual companies to recapture the financing function of your life. This is not about a product — it's a way of thinking about how you use money.

The Nash Method — How It Works

Four steps to your
private banking system

1

Design the Policy

We design a dividend-paying whole life policy from a mutual company — structured with paid-up additions to maximize early cash value, following Nelson Nash Institute guidelines.

2

Fund Consistently

Premiums flow into your policy, building guaranteed, uninterruptible cash value that grows tax-deferred — not tied to any market index.

3

Borrow Against It

Access your cash value through policy loans — instantly, without credit checks, without interrupting growth. Your money never leaves the policy.

4

Repay & Repeat

Repay the loan on your schedule, recapturing the interest that would have gone to a bank — then repeat. This is the banking cycle Nash describes.

Why Nash's Approach Works

Why IBC changes
the financial equation

📈

Guaranteed Growth

Dividend-paying whole life from a mutual company grows at a guaranteed rate plus non-guaranteed dividends — completely disconnected from market volatility.

🏦

Access Without Permission

No bank approval. No credit inquiry. As Nash taught, you become the banker — accessing capital on your terms, for any purpose you choose.

🛡️

Creditor Protected

In most states, life insurance cash values enjoy significant protection from creditors and lawsuits — a key pillar of financial sovereignty.

🌿

Tax-Advantaged

Cash value grows tax-deferred. Policy loans are generally income-tax-free. Death benefit passes income-tax-free — triple tax advantages in one vehicle.

💰

Recapture the Banking Function

Nash's core insight: you finance everything you buy — either paying interest to others or giving up interest you could have earned. IBC recaptures that function.

🏛️

Multi-Generational Legacy

The death benefit passes income-tax-free, and the banking system itself can be passed to the next generation — exactly as Nash envisioned.
